About Me

Learn more about my background, my path into education, and my passion for computer science.

Who I Am

My name is Robin Kelley, and I am passionate about education, technology, and helping others grow through learning. I am currently building my future as a computer science educator while continuing to expand my own knowledge and experience in the field.

As a first-generation college student and mother, education has been an important part of my personal and professional journey. My experiences have shaped the way I approach teaching, learning, and problem solving.

My Academic Journey

I am attending Arkansas Tech University and working toward a degree focused on becoming a computer science educator. My goal is to combine technical knowledge with strong instructional skills so I can support the next generation of students in technology.

I believe computer science should be approachable, engaging, and meaningful for all learners, and I hope to create classroom experiences that encourage confidence, curiosity, and persistence.

Why Computer Science Education Matters to Me

I want to be more than just a teacher who delivers content. I want to be an educator who helps students understand how technology connects to real-world problem solving, creativity, and opportunity.

My goal is to help students feel capable in computer science, whether they are brand new to the subject or already exploring advanced concepts.

Education

Experience

Bringing together teaching experience, technical knowledge, and previous professional work in data analysis.

Goal

To inspire and prepare students for success in computer science and technology-related careers.
